# v4.3 Content Draft Brief

> Scope: a *writing brief*, not a final page. The brief sets the tone, the hero draft, the 3-minute guide draft, the section draft briefs, and the artifact card writing brief. The future build round uses this brief as a starting point; v4.3 does not write to `site/`.
>
> The status `approved` is **not used**. The phrase "safe for commercial use" is **not used**. The drafts are *not* final page text; they are starting points.

---

## Tone

- **Chinese.** All copy is in Chinese (Simplified). The English working title is a reference, not a translation.
- **Research-grade but accessible to general visitors.** The exhibition is for a museum-going public, not a specialist audience. Every term used in the copy is also in the glossary.
- **No jargon stacking.** Each section introduces at most 2–3 new terms. The glossary carries the rest.
- **Every image returns to a question.** Each artifact card answers one of the four section questions: 观察 (what the illustrator chose to look at), 分类 (how the image supports a name), 复制 (how the medium changes the image), 再组织 (how a digital collection re-links the image).
- **No fake reverence.** The copy is not a eulogy for the work; it is a description of the *operation* the work performs.

---

## Hero draft (150–220 Chinese characters)

> 一张植物图像，从田野到书页、从书页到数据库，经历了什么？这次展览挑选了六件作品，沿「观察—分类—复制—再组织」四步走：先看一份前人怎么把植物画下来；再看一份植物学名如何与一份标本绑在一起；再比较同一张图在书籍、版画、扫描中的不同呈现；最后看一份 IIIF 清单如何让同一株植物重新连上别处的标本与别处的记录。每一步的图像都来自可追溯的公共馆藏，文字只描述图像在做什么。

(186 Chinese characters. Subject: a botanical image's journey; argument: the four-step path; promise: each image comes from a traceable public collection; tone: descriptive, not reverent.)

---

## 3-minute guide draft (4–5 points)

1. **观察。一张植物图像是一份「决定」**——谁画的、画给谁看、画了哪个角度、留了哪片叶，都是选择。看一份 BHL 的前 1928 年公共域植物图版，识别它「决定」留下了什么。
2. **分类。一张植物图如何变成「名字」**——同一株植物在史密森尼 NMNH 的标本上和在一份植物学名册上指向同一处。看一份 NMNH 公共域 (CC0) 标本片，理解「名 + 标 + 图」三位一体。
3. **复制。一张图如何被复制又改变**——同一块版面在不同时代用不同工艺复刻。看一份 BHL 公共域书页，对比原版与扫描版本的差异。
4. **再组织。一张图如何被数字馆藏重新连接**——同一件作品在 IIIF 清单上同时连着原馆、别馆、别条目。看一份 Rijksmuseum 的 IIIF 清单，理解清单本身就是一张网。
5. **回到「为什么」**——每张图都不是孤立的「画」。它是一段决策、一段命名、一段复制、一段再连接。

(The 3-minute guide is 5 points; each point maps to a section + a candidate. The future build round will polish the wording and may break the points into 5 cards on the page.)

## Section draft briefs

Each section's brief covers: core question, content focus, candidate linkage, viewer takeaway, glossary terms.

### Section 1 — 观察

- **Core question.** 一张植物图像如何从一次观察变成一次决定？
- **Content focus.** The act of *selecting* what to depict. The plate is not a neutral copy of the plant; it is a curatorial argument.
- **Candidate linkage.** **C-01** (BHL PD plate) primary; **C-09** (Rijksmuseum) cross-section alternate.
- **Viewer takeaway.** 看完这张图，你应该能说出「这张图的画家决定看什么、决定不画什么」。
- **Glossary terms.** botanical illustration, plate, engraving, public domain.

### Section 2 — 分类

- **Core question.** 一张植物图像如何把一株植物变成一个「名字」？
- **Content focus.** The herbarium sheet is the unit of classification: name + specimen + image, all on one page. The label is the *contract* between the name and the specimen.
- **Candidate linkage.** **C-06** (Smithsonian NMNH CC0) primary; **C-09** cross-section alternate.
- **Viewer takeaway.** 看完这张图，你应该能说出「为什么这一份标本可以被叫作这个名字」。
- **Glossary terms.** herbarium, specimen, taxonomy, metadata, accession number.

### Section 3 — 复制

- **Core question.** 一张植物图像在被复制的过程中失去了什么、增加了什么？
- **Content focus.** The same plate re-appears in book form, in lithograph form, in scan form. Each medium adds or removes something (color, scale, accessibility, shareability).
- **Candidate linkage.** **C-03** (BHL PD book page, **PD subset only**) primary; **C-08** (Met) alternate.
- **Viewer takeaway.** 看完这张图，你应该能说出「这本书页和原图在哪些细节上不同」。
- **Glossary terms.** digitization, plate, lithograph, public domain, collection record.

### Section 4 — 再组织

- **Core question.** 一份数字馆藏如何让同一张图重新连上别处？
- **Content focus.** A IIIF manifest is not just an image; it is a node in a network. The same image, exposed via IIIF, can be re-linked to other records, other editions, other specimens.
- **Candidate linkage.** **C-10** (Rijksmuseum IIIF) primary.
- **Viewer takeaway.** 看完这张图，你应该能说出「这份清单在不在网上？同一份图还能连到什么？」
- **Glossary terms.** IIIF manifest, persistent identifier, digitization, metadata, collection record.

## Cross-section writing notes

- **Every section body returns to a single question.** The section body is a paragraph, not a list. The list structure is for the artifact cards and the 3-minute guide, not the section body.
- **Every artifact card cites its source.** The source note is a separate field on the card; the credit line is the visible attribution.
- **No "approved" language.** The cards do not say "this image is approved for use". They say "this image is from [institution], under [licence]".
- **The curatorial essay is a future round.** v4.3 does not draft it.
